Great Auk

First Described 1758

The Great Auk formerly known as (Pingiuinus Impennis) is a species of flightless northern atlantic bird closely related to modern penguins. The species has largely been thought to be extinct since the 1700-1800s however there had been unconfirmed sightings of the bird as recently as 2010. Today if they are still out there they are mostly in very small populations so keep an eye out for an 75 to 85 centimeter tall bird with white and black feathers…ALSO don’t freeze to death in the altantic.
